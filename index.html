The Maker

A self-taught
craftsman from
Iloilo.

Arnel John "Nonoy" Vallejo began making pens as a personal passion — a way to hold something, feel its weight, and understand what it means to put Filipino hands to Filipino materials. What started as a side project became a calling.

Today, Banwa Pens is one of the Philippines' most internationally recognized artisan pen brands — carried into diplomatic summits, collected worldwide, and rooted in the belief that craft is a form of cultural storytelling.

"Banwa" — an old Visayan word for community, homeland, nation.
Every pen carries that meaning

How It's Made

Every pen is
made once.

i.
Selecting the blank
Each commission begins with the material — sourced from Philippine forests, farms, and workshops. Nonoy selects each blank by hand, reading the grain for what it wants to become.
ii.
Turning on the lathe
The blank is mounted and shaped entirely by hand on a manual lathe. No CNC. No template. The form emerges through judgment, pressure, and patience accumulated over thousands of hours.
iii.
Finishing and polish
Multiple stages of hand-sanding, from coarse grits down to micromesh, followed by hand-buffing. The final surface reflects light the way no factory process can replicate.
iv.
Nib fitting and testing
Premium Bock or JoWo nibs are fitted, adjusted, and tested by writing. A Banwa fountain pen doesn't leave the workshop until it writes the way it should.
v.
Delivery
Your pen is packaged, documented, and shipped. If commissioned as a gift, Nonoy can include a handwritten card — written, naturally, with a Banwa pen.
